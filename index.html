Papers

Welcome! I am a second-year Ph.D. student in the Ubiquitous Computing Lab at the University of Washington, advised by Vikram Iyer and Shwetak Patel.

My research focuses on two (as of right now) separate domains:

  1. Developing sustainable computing systems that demonstrate sensing, networking, and actuation, and more despite tight power, material, and form-factor constraints.
  2. Exploring interaction techniques for Large Language Models (LLMs). Specifically, I'm working on tenchniques to synthesize useful explanations of diverse mobile health data as well as identifying ways to apply LLMs to aid in the development of embedded hardware-software systems.

Check back in a few years to see how I've managed to bridge the gap between these two areas...

If anything I'm working on seems interesting to you, please get in touch!
